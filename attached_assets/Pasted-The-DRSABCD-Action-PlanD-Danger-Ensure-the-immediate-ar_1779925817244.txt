The DRSABCD Action PlanD - Danger: Ensure the immediate area is safe for yourself, bystanders, and the patient. Do not put yourself at risk.R - Response: Check if the person is responsive. Ask for their name and squeeze their shoulders gently.S - Send for Help: Call Triple Zero (000) in Australia, or your local emergency number, immediately. Send a bystander to find an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) if nearby.A - Airway: Open their mouth to check for any foreign objects. If clear, tilt their head back and lift their chin (head tilt/chin lift) to open the airway. If foreign material is present, roll the casualty onto their side (recovery position) and clear their airway.B - Breathing: Look, listen, and feel for normal breathing for exactly 10 seconds.If they are breathing normally, place them in the recovery position (on their side) and monitor them.If they are not breathing normally, or gasping, they need immediate CPR.C - CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation): If the patient is unresponsive and not breathing normally, begin CPR immediately.Start with 30 chest compressions in the center of the chest.Compress at a rate of \(100\) to \(120\) compressions per minute.Give 2 rescue breaths (if you are trained and willing).Repeat the 30:2 cycle until an ambulance arrives, an AED is ready to use, or the person regains consciousness.D - Defibrillator: As soon as an AED is available, turn it on and follow the automated voice prompts while continuing CPR.Next Steps for Specific EmergenciesOnce the primary life-support steps are under control, St John provides specialized step-by-step procedures depending on the situation:Severe Bleeding: Apply firm, direct pressure to the wound. Raise the injured limb if possible and apply a sterile dressing or bandage.Burns: Cool the burn immediately under cold running water for a minimum of \(20\) minutes. Do not apply ice or harsh ointments. Cover with a clean, loose, non-stick dressing.Anaphylaxis / Severe Allergy: Follow the person’s personal action plan, which usually involves administering an adrenaline auto-injector (e.g., EpiPen) into the mid-outer thigh and calling 000 immediately.Shock: Lay the casualty down (if comfortable), elevate their legs slightly (unless they have a leg injury), keep them warm with a blanket, and offer calm reassurance
